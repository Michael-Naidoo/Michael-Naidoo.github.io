Quantifying Latent Space Entropy in Generative Adversarial Networks

By: [Your Name] and Dr. A. K. Varma

The first quantifiable metric for measuring the structural complexity of high-dimensional latent spaces, improving GAN fidelity and stability.

Download Full PDF

Abstract

Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) often suffer from mode collapse, a phenomenon attributed to insufficient exploration of the latent space $Z$. This paper introduces the concept of Latent Space Entropy (LSE), a novel metric derived from kernel density estimation, which provides a numerical measure of the complexity and diversity of the learned manifold. We demonstrate that maximizing LSE during training correlates strongly with improved Fr√©chet Inception Distance (FID) scores and increased sample variability. Experiments on CIFAR-10 and LSUN datasets validate the efficacy of LSE as an objective function stabilizer for both $\mathcal{W}$-GAN and StyleGAN architectures.

Research Methodology & Technical Insights

Background & Problem Statement

Current evaluation metrics for generative models focus primarily on output quality (e.g., FID, IS) but fail to diagnose internal model stability or latent space geometry. This leads to opaque training processes where mode collapse is often detected too late. Our goal was to create a real-time, differentiable metric to guide the latent space towards a more uniform and expressive distribution.

The mathematical foundation involves applying Shannon Entropy concepts to the mapped latent vectors $\mathbf{z} \in Z$ after passing through the generator's initial layers. This required implementing a custom, vectorized batch estimator to maintain computational efficiency during training. This technique is easily transferable to any deep learning framework.

Methodology Highlights

LSE Metric Implementation

Development of the core Latent Space Entropy function using PyTorch and customized CUDA kernels for speed.

Objective Function Integration

Incorporating the LSE as an auxiliary loss term $\mathcal{L}_{\text{LSE}}$ to penalize low-entropy latent distributions.

Empirical Validation

Testing against five baseline GAN architectures on standard computer vision benchmarks (CIFAR-10, CelebA).

Visualization & Analysis

t-SNE and PCA visualizations confirming the broader dispersion of latent clusters when LSE is maximized.
