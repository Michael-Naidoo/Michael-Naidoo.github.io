Game Overview & Development Insights

The Vision: Precision, Strategy, and Dynamic Flow

Samurai Senshi is designed around the concept of fluid aggression and immediate tactical choices. The game features 4 unique Samurai characters and 4 distinct weapons (e.g., Katana, Daggar, Mace, Bow). Each character is proficient with two of these weapons, allowing players to instantly shift their stance and move-set mid-combat.

The biggest design breakthrough was the non-pausing buff system. When a player reaches a specific low-health threshold, they are presented with two permanant buffs (e.g., increased attack speed, decreased stamina cost) to choose from, all while the action continues. This forces high-stakes, quick decision-making that rewards mastery under pressure, keeping the competitive flow unbroken.

Key Game Features

4 Samurai, 2 Weapons Each

Master the unique combinations of the four warriors and their assigned weapon pairs for unmatched flexibility.

Dynamic Mid-Fight Buffs (New System)

Non-interrupting buff selection when near death, replacing the old, disruptive pause system to maintain fight momentum.

Instant Weapon Switching

Swap between the two assigned weapons at will, blending different attack ranges and utility for complex combos.

Frame Data Precision

Developed using meticulous frame data analysis to ensure competitive balance and high-level technical play.

Technical Specifications

  • Engine: Unity
  • Primary Tools: Unity, Krita, Custom Physics Engine (C#)
  • Combat System: State Machines, Frame-Perfect Hitbox Detection
  • Duration: 9 months (Team Development)
  • Current Status: Alpha 0.9 (Looking for Testers)

Challenges Solved

Developing the non-pausing UI overlay for the buff selection was the main technical hurdle, requiring careful handling of game state and input prioritization during active combat.
